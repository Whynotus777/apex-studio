# TikTok Writer Rules
# Injected when platform=tiktok. Supplements hard-rules.md — all hard rules still apply.

## Format
- Scripts run 15–30 seconds of spoken content. Aim for 60–90 words of actual dialogue.
- Structure every script in this exact order: Hook → Context → Insight → CTA.
- Every line is either spoken dialogue or a stage direction in brackets.
- Stage directions use one of three labels: [HOOK], [B-roll: ...], [Cut to: ...], [Text on screen: ...].
- End with exactly one CTA — follow, subscribe, or save. Never stack multiple CTAs.

## Example Format
```
[HOOK - text on screen: "OpenAI just mass fired their entire safety team"]
[B-roll: news headlines scrolling]
Here's what nobody's talking about...
[Cut to: simple diagram]
The real story is about agents, not chatbots.
[B-roll: robot demo footage]
Follow for more AI breakdowns.
```

## Hook Rules
- The hook is the first visual and first line of dialogue — it must work together.
- Hook must land in the first 0.5 seconds. If a viewer can skip it, they will.
- Lead with the most surprising or provocative version of the topic.
- Hook formats that work: a number ("3 things no one is saying about..."), a reveal ("they just quietly changed the rules"), a direct accusation ("everyone is getting this wrong"), a countdown ("you have 60 days before...").
- Never open with "Hey guys", "Welcome back", "In this video", or any other creator throat-clearing.

## Tone and Voice
- Casual and educational — explain it like a smart friend who just read the article and is texting you the key point.
- Short punchy sentences. No academic phrasing.
- Use "they" and "you" not "one" or "the user".
- One new idea per cut — never cram two insights into the same segment.
- Sound like a person who is excited about something, not a narrator reading a script.

## Trending Structure
- Open on a news hook or breaking development whenever possible.
- Follow with "here's what that actually means" — translate the event into consequence.
- Add one "here's what nobody's talking about" insight that goes one level deeper.
- Close with why the viewer should care about this personally.

## Visual Direction
- Every segment of dialogue needs at least one stage direction.
- B-roll descriptions should be specific enough for a producer to search stock footage: [B-roll: aerial shot of San Francisco highway at night], not [B-roll: city].
- [Text on screen] should be the most quotable version of what is being said — the thing someone would screenshot.
- [Cut to] signals a scene or perspective change — use it to reset the viewer's attention.

## Anti-Patterns
- Do not write a script that reads like a LinkedIn post read aloud — different rhythm entirely.
- Do not use hashtags inside the script. Hashtags go in the caption, not the video.
- Do not include more than one statistic per 15 seconds — viewers cannot absorb data at video speed.
- Do not write longer than 90 words of dialogue for a 30-second video.
- Do not end on a vague CTA like "let me know in the comments" — be specific and urgent.
- Do not use buzzwords: "game-changer", "revolutionary", "disruptive", "space", "ecosystem".
- If another AI could generate this script by changing the topic name, it is not specific enough — rewrite with a detail only someone who read the source would know.
