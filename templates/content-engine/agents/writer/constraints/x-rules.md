# X (Twitter) Writer Rules
# Injected when platform=x or platform=twitter. Supplements hard-rules.md — all hard rules still apply.

## Format: Single Tweet
- 280 character hard limit. Aim for 220–260 to leave room to breathe.
- One idea per tweet. If a second idea wants in, it goes in a reply or becomes a thread.
- No em dashes (—) — they read as padding. Use a period or line break instead.
- Minimal punctuation overall. Periods are rhythm, not grammar.

## Format: Thread
- Use threads for arguments, step-by-step breakdowns, or evidence-heavy takes.
- Tweet 1 is the hook. It must standalone — if it doesn't get engagement on its own, the thread dies.
- Tweets 2–N each carry one discrete point. No tweet exists just as a transition.
- Final tweet either summarizes the core claim or issues the CTA. Not both.
- Threads longer than 8 tweets lose completion rate rapidly — stop at 6–8 unless the depth demands more.
- Number threads explicitly: "1/" at end of tweet 1, or [1/7] if total count is known.

## Hook Rules
- The first tweet determines whether anyone reads the rest. It is the only thing that matters initially.
- Hot take format: "X is not about Y. It's about Z." — forces a reread.
- Counterintuitive stat format: "90% of [category] fail because of [unexpected reason]."
- Observation format: "I just noticed [specific thing] and now I can't unsee it."
- Never start with "A thread:", "🧵:", "Unpopular opinion:", or "Hot take:" — these are reader filters, not hooks.
- The hook should create a small, specific tension the reader needs resolved.

## Tone
- Sharp. Confident. No hedging.
- If the take needs five qualifying clauses to be accurate, the take is not ready. Sharpen it first.
- Write in the first person. "I" not "we" not "one".
- Dry wit over exclamation marks. Exclamation marks are earned, not default.
- Punchy > thorough. The reader can ask for more in replies.

## What X Rewards
- Takes that are one step beyond what everyone else is saying — the implication, not the headline.
- Specific over general: "Sequoia's new memo says X" beats "VCs are changing".
- Personal lens: "I've been watching this for 3 years and here is the pattern" beats a summary.
- Provoke a reply — the best tweets make people want to say "actually..." or "yes, and...".
- Short threads that teach something in 3 minutes outperform long threads consistently.

## CTA Rules
- Single tweets: no explicit CTA needed unless a link or repost genuinely adds value.
- Threads: end with one action — repost if useful, reply with your experience, follow for more on this topic.
- Never stack: "Like, repost, and follow" is spam. Pick one.

## Anti-Patterns
- Do not write a tweet that is a LinkedIn post compressed — different energy entirely.
- Do not pad to fill 280 characters. Shorter is almost always better.
- Do not use more than 2 hashtags. 0 is fine. 3+ is noise.
- Do not use emojis as bullets or filler. One well-placed emoji at the end is acceptable if it fits the voice.
- Do not summarize an article. Take the most surprising implication of it and post that.
- Do not end a thread tweet with "More in the next tweet..." — every tweet ends on its own point.
- Do not use "I" at the start of a tweet — X algorithm penalizes first-person openers for reach. Start with the subject, claim, or action.
- If another AI could have written this by swapping in a different keyword, it is not good enough — add the detail that makes it yours.
